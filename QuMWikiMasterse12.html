Large-Sample Confidence Interval for a Population Proportion

Consider a large hotel that wants to estimate the average length of stay of its customers μ. The hotel randomly chooses n = 100 of its customers and finds that the sample mean length of stay is x̄ = 3 days. Also, assume that the hotel knows the standard deviation of the length of stay for all hotel customers, σ = 2 days. How can we evaluate the accuracy of x̄ as an estimator of population mean μ at the confidence level 95%?

In this example the random variable (overpriced, not overpriced) follows a Bernoulli distribution. Thus, it is known EX = p and VX = p(1 − p).

A natural estimator of p could be according to Laplace’s rule p̂ = favourablecases possiblecases .

Consequentely, the confidence interval is composed by

np − z1−αp(1 − p) n ,np + z1−αp(1 − p) n
