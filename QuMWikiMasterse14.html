2.7 QuM2 – Session 7 (Inference Based on Two Samples)

Let the body heights of the people in one population be normal distributed with μ1 = 170 and σ1 = 7.5, and in the other population be normal distributed with μ2 = 180 and σ2 = 7.5.

mu.population.1 <- 170
sigma.population.1 <- 7.5
mu.population.2 <- 180
sigma.population.2 <- 7.5

Two samples are drawn out of this population.

set.seed(1007)
sample.1 <- rnorm(50, mean=mu.population.1, sd=sigma.population.1)
sample.2 <- rnorm(50, mean=mu.population.2, sd=sigma.population.2)

There are two main methods for analysing the samples to get inference on the relationship of their means depending on the background of the data.

If the two samples are independent arising from two unrelated populations with the same variances, then the two samples do not affect each other. and a t-test for independent samples can be used. In case of large sample sizes, i.,e. n1 ≫ 30 and n2 ≫ 30, the variances σ12 and σ22 do not have to be equal. For smaller sample sizes a t-test must not be performed if the variances σ12 and σ22 are not equal. In this case a Welch-test has to be used.

Note: Despite σ1 and σ2 are unknown, σ1 −σ2 = 0 is known.
In case the sample size is small the formulas for the t-value are the same for known and unknown standard deviations.

If the two samples are pairwise dependent, e. g. coming from two different surveys on the same individuals, then it must not be conducted a t-test for independent samples. In this case a paired t-test has to be performed. It is the same test as a t-test for the sample of differences, which have to be normally distributed for a small sample size n ≤ 30.

So assume now that the body heights of people of two different islands are compared. This is the result:

t.test(
  sample.1, sample.2,
  alternative=c("two.sided", "less", "greater"),
  mu=0,
  paired=FALSE, var.equal=TRUE,
  conf.level=0.95
)
##  
##  Two Sample t-test  
##  
## data:  sample.1 and sample.2  
## t = -8.3542, df = 98, p-value = 4.425e-13  
## alternative hypothesis: true difference in means is not equal to 0  
## 95 percent confidence interval:  
##  -14.032965  -8.645841  
## sample estimates:  
## mean of x mean of y  
##  170.2163  181.5557

In another case, teenagers are measured and a few years later the same people are measured again. The two samples are not independent any more. Therefore, a paired t-test has to be used.

In both of the tests, the interpretation of the results is like in the one-sample t-test.

If σ1 − σ2 = 0 is not known, then the parameter var.equal has to be set to FALSE (by default).
